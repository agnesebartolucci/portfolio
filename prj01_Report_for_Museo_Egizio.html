Integrated report of Museo Egizio

The Museo Egizio (Egyptian Museum) is a museum in Turin specilizing in Egyptian archaeology and anthropology. The institution asked Accurat to design its first integrated report. They intended to create an innovative document focused on what generates value for the museum through high-impact graphics. The report is divided into four sections, each characterized by a color of the brand identity. The colors are used again in the graphic elements within each one of them, to favor a strong characterization and visual clarity. It was decided to create impactful visualizations, which were the protagonists of the pages but also immediately legible. For this reason, the legend is often in the infographic itself, and the colors are few and solid.

MY CONTRIBUTION
This report was the last project I worked on at Accurat, and was released a few months after my internship ended. For this, I have only contributed to the early stages of the process: the most conceptual and creative part. I helped to collect references, experiment with various styles, specify the first visual guidelines, and, in general, discuss the direction to take in every aspect. The parts I worked on most were the timelines, the tables, and the first drafts for the visualizations outside the highlights section.
