Esc

→ book of the project

The project aimed to create an interactive narrative—a digital experience where users influence a storyline through their actions—by transmedially translating the novel Petronius by Marcel Schwob. Hence, ”Esc” is a Location-Based Mobile Game (LBMG) composed of physical and digital media, designed to encourage university students to explore the university area and experience it as a vibrant neighborhood with places waiting to be discovered.

Players, assuming the role of the main character, utilize an app and a daily wrecking journal to immerse themselves in the narrative, interacting with it through meaningful choices that dictate different routes and endings. The theme of discovery, central to the original work, is reimagined using escape as a metaphor, encouraging users to explore their surroundings and engage with physical environments. This theme is emphasized throughout, guiding users to uncover hidden gems in the city's often-overlooked areas.

The narrative unfolds in episodes, where the protagonist's dialogues and actions, involving choices or actions by the users, are pivotal for the story's progression. In various locations, users encounter artifacts crucial for advancing the game.

2019 — University project

Team: Leonardo Codamo, Anna Gazza, Riccardo Negretti, Eleonora Rossetti, Ruoquing Shen, Barbara Vanoli, Junpeng Zhao

Faculty: Mariana Ciancia, Ilaria Mariani

Skills: Game Design, Graphic Design, Visual Design, Editorial Design, UX/UI Design, Prototyping, Brand Identity

My contribution: working on "Esc" was incredibly enjoyable, especially since it was set in our university neighborhood. I contributed to the concept and storytelling, developed and wrote lines for a character, and devised all the routes in the neighborhood. Regarding graphic design, I played a role in shaping the project's visual identity. Additionally, I crafted various artifacts for the journal and riddles, such as maps, articles, and postcards. Furthermore, I handled the on-site aspects, designing every poster, flyer, or sticker the user encounters along the way.

"Esc" was developed to uphold the original values, themes, and main characters of the story while also introducing new elements to expand the narrative universe. The game adapts the novel into an alternate reality, preserving the main characters but renaming them as Petronio and Sirio. The use of the journal medium mirrors Petronius' passion for writing. Central to the story is the theme of the desire to escape to discover something new, meet new people, and embark on new adventures. This theme is emphasized throughout the narrative, serving as its guiding principle.

↑ The character cards offer additional information and help users better connect with them.

The story is set in a mildly dystopian Italy in 2020, following a fictional educational reform enacted in 2002. The university gained autonomy in self-regulation, establishing a system where discipline, rigor, and seriousness reign as central values. It shapes students' educational paths and aims to standardize their thinking. This environment starkly contrasts with the suburbs, where self-expression and diversity are celebrated.

↑ The map included in the journal serves to facilitate the user's exploration of the various locations.

The game prototype is set in Bovisa, Milan. However, "ESC" is designed to be adaptable to various university areas, employing a multimedia narrative supported by physical and digital media to connect the story to the physical environment where users engage. The game prompts users to interact with physical locations through actions and artifacts that advance the narrative.

The project comprises a system of artifacts, beginning with the advertising campaign. Following this is the journal, which users utilize to record vital information, solve riddles and minigames, access a map for navigation, and consult rules governing the use of provided media. Additionally, the visual novel takes the form of a mobile app, employing a geolocation system to unlock various scenes. Each scene features photos, dialogues, and multiple choices leading to different endings. Supplementary content, including newspaper articles, character cards, and Instagram profiles, enhances user immersion in the narrative.

↑ Nearly every location features a riddle that users must solve to uncover the next destination, though it may not directly impact the storyline. Alternatively, some locations host minigames, where successful completion is pivotal for advancing the narrative.

↑ The advertising posters are designed to be displayed within the game's setting: the university and its surrounding areas that players can explore.
