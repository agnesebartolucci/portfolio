Non sono solo immagini

→ original thesis (IT) | translated thesis (EN)

In the last years, the way people communicate has radically transformed: thanks to digital technologies, new relational possibilities and new forms of violence have opened up. One of them is image-based sexual abuse, the non-consensual distribution and/or creation of private sexual material. This type of violence is perpetrated daily within vast online communities to the detriment of mainly unaware victims.

My research aimed to study the practices of image-based sexual abuse and the role of platforms in their implementation within the community, focusing not on the victims but on the perpetrators. The opportunity I identified to obtain this shift in perspective (from victims to perpetrators) is that of online vernacular language, the set of expressions and styles peculiar to digital communities.

“Non sono solo immagini” is a the website I designed to educate and raise awareness on image-based sexual abuse using the evidence from the research carried out on online vernacular language.

The website is a collection of artifacts displayed as data visualizations, designed to analyze the communications and practices of those who perpetrate violence. Its aim is to provide the user with tools to initiate a critical reflection, serving as the first step toward social change.

↑ Due to the sensitive nature of the content, the website begins with a trigger warning, alerting users to the presence of sexual violence and derogatory and violent language.

↑ The homepage of the website features a brief introduction to image-based sexual abuse and its practices.

The catalogue is the first artifact, it displays 313 requests of images and/or videos that constitute image-based sexual abuse made by the members of 4 Telegram groups active in the Italian pornography subculture.

The requests have been analyzed and visually rendered as postcards. These are displayed as a visualization, where the filter buttons also function as a legend.

The background color represents the proposed action, like material exchange or discussion. Each solid shape represents the subject requested, whether it's a partner, ex, or parent, with the shape's color denoting the subject's gender. The outlined shape, if specified, symbolizes the content genre, like rape or child pornography.

The glossary is the second artifact, it displays the most frequently used terms by 4chan users when requesting the manipulation of material to make it sexually explicit.

The various types of manipulations are presented like a card index, with each manipulation as a file containing its definition, variants, related terms, and examples. This index also functions as a word cloud, organizing terms based on their frequency of use.

The color of each file represents the type of manipulation requested. For instance, one may ask to alter the subject, relocate it in another image, or perform a specific action within the image.

↑ Overview of both artifacts.

The theoretical background of image-based sexual abuse, the research I conducted on 4chan and Telegram using digital methods, and the concept and design of "Non sono solo immagini" are explained in detail in the thesis book.

I designed five research protocols utilizing digital methods to conduct investigations on 4chan and Telegram. Specifically, for 4chan, I concentrated on the /r/ board, analyzing the most requested items and those garnering the highest interactions. This examination revealed that "image manipulation" was the most sought-after request, as depicted in the following visualization.

For my second research protocol, I delved into the language used in requests for image manipulation, resulting in a visualization spotlighting the most frequently sought manipulations. This visualization ultimately informed the glossary featured in “Non sono solo immagini.”

The third research protocol was designed for Telegram. I targeted content linked to specific keywords, including terms related to women ('women' and 'girls') and gender-based violence ('rape'). This process uncovered a roster of Telegram channels linked to pornography. Building on this, the fourth research protocol entailed cataloging these entities (channels, groups, etc.) and the links they shared, effectively mapping the pornographic subculture within Italian Telegram. The resulting visualization portrays this map.

Given that Telegram groups are often hubs for image-based sexual abuse, my fifth and final research protocol focused on these groups. I scraped their textual messages and analyzed every request made more than five times, categorizing them by content, as illustrated in the following visualization. Ultimately, this process contributed to the catalogue included in “Non sono solo immagini.”
