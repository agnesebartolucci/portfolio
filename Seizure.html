Seizure

Epilepsy, a widespread neurological disorder, remains incompletely understood due to its complex manifestations. We developed this data-driven poster generator in Kotlin, using rndr.studio, to explore this topic through direct witness accounts.

We collected and analyzed statements describing seizure experiences, and divided them into six metaphors that are visually represented by the alteration of the text. The poster also depicts other symptoms extracted from direct witnesses' accounts such as spasms, loss of muscular control, loss of consciousness, and the perception of electric shock. Users can manipulate the poster's appearance based on these criteria to explore the complexity and disruptive nature of seizures.

2020 — University project

Team: Antonella Autuori, Elena Catani, Lucia Ferrari, Anna Gazza, Barbara Vanoli

Faculty: Edwin Jakobs

Skills: Information Design, Visual Design, Qualitative Research, Data Analysis

My contribution: this project was developed during a one-week workshop, placing our group under considerable pressure. My main contributions involved research, data analysis, conceptualization, and programming text editing functions.

The poster generator emphasizes the uniqueness of each seizure, allowing users to adjust the intensity of the alterations.

↑ The six identified metaphors are crawling, electricity, oppression, overpower, space, and vortex. Each element in the poster symbolizes a specific symptom. White represents the progressive loss of consciousness, while blue symbolizes the electricity from the overload of brain activity.
