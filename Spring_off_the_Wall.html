Event Design, Communication Campaign

Spring Off the Wall

Vans is a unique brand in the sportswear world; its values grounds in youth, authenticity, and individual style, due, among others thing, to their relation with the table sports environment. Their goal is to encourage customers to think differently. For this reason, they define their approach "Off the Wall". For Milan Design Week 2018, a workshop for students was held by the Vans EMEA Team and the Politecnico di Milano with the aim to ideate an event that could convey the values of the brand. The result was a space divided into four areas, the first to dive in the Vans world and the others based on a feeling that skaters can experience while practicing (vertigo, balance, and speed). Some of the Communication Design students were in charge of creating the communication system of the event by merging some of the ideas presented in the first week of the workshop.

For the communication campaign, three symbols inspired by skating, both in 2D and 3D, were created based on each sensation. A skate legend and Vans' ambassador was associated with each feeling: Tony Alva for balance, Steve Caballero for vertigo, and Christian Hosoi for speed. These were the main elements used for the artifacts, starting from the poster to the three flyers. Red-colored stickers were created for a guerrilla marketing operation in Milan, together with those of the ambassadors findable in shops.

The event's information was shared on Vans' official channels and in the Milano Design Week's main ones, such as Fuorisalone.it.

Vans EMEA Team gave freedom to students' decisions, and this collaboration culminated in an active presence during the event.

During the first week of the workshop, groups of students ideated six projects. I was on a team with Stefano Bracchi, Federica Buratti, Elena Catani, Leonardo Codamo, Nicolò Falzoni, Davide Formenti, Caterina Mandas, Andrea Rigamonti, Eleonora Rossetti, Giacomo Zambon, and our project, Feel Like Vans, was used as a base for some parts of the event.

MY CONTRIBUTION
During the first week of the workshop, I helped conceive the three experiences we proposed for the event. My role, however, was mainly to design the communication campaign and graphic identity. Instead, during the development of the actual project, I have contributed to the design and creation of the communication campaign, helping to combine and homogenise ideas from different projects. I also worked as a project manager and managed communication with the company. Finally, I worked on reception during Design Week.
