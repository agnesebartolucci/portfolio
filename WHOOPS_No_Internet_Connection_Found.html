WHOOPS! No Internet Connection Found

→ visit the website | download the visualization

An internet shutdown is a disruption of internet communications caused by technical problems or, more often, a government decision to exert control over specific events.

The infographic presents this phenomenon, analyzing the total outages that occurred in 2018. It showcases the location, quantity, duration, and causes (both suspected and declared) of the shutdowns. The visualization includes a further level of interpretation: the democracy index of each country. This additional layer helps understand if there is a connection between the type of government and the frequency of internet shutdowns.

2020 — University project

Team: Antonella Autuori, Elena Catani, Mattia Cittadino, Anna Gazza, Barbara Vanoli

Faculty: Ángeles Briones, Gabriele Colombo, Michele Mauri, Simone Vantini, Salvatore Zingale

Skills: Data Visualization Design, Information Design, Graphic Design, Data Analysis

My contribution: I contributed to reaching the final result by experimenting with various visualization models and visual styles. Additionally, I was involved in researching, analyzing, and organizing a portion of the data.

"WHOOPS! No internet connection found" only depicts the total outages in 2018, wherein a total internet shutdown occurs when all internet services are completely blocked. The omitted countries either did not experience any internet shutdowns or did not release any data regarding them.

↑ The infographic underwent several stages of design, starting with a grid that served as the foundation for organizing the different modules representing each shutdown.
