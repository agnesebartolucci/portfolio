Law of the Jungle

→ website

Instagram proposes itself as an open-minded platform, but through the use of policies about sexual content, it denies its users' freedom of expression, reinforcing the taboos of our society. Is it so necessary to censor these types of images? Moreover, the community guidelines are somewhat ambiguous, so the users do not fully understand what they can post or not, which sometimes leads to removed images or blocked accounts. The aim is creating an experience that lets the users know the community guidelines of Instagram, highlighting the aspects that could be unclear, ambiguous, or debatable. The project uses absurdity and irony to make the ambiguity more visible. Therefore, it starts with a strong provocation: what would happen if the Instagram nudity policy applied to the entire animal world? Users adapted to the policies to survive on the platform, so how would Instagram deal with the variety of species, shapes, and habits of the animal world?

"Law of the Jungle" is a website where the users play as content moderators, helping Instagram to censor inadequate photos and to recognise explicit images of humans and other animals. The experience is a game made of eight quizzes, two for each theme found in the guidelines, which gradually reveals the Instagram policy about sexual content. The ambiguity of the vast numbers of images to censor helps users reflect on the topic.

↑ The tasks of each level use a specific section of the policy to facilitate the user to perform them.

↑ At the end of the tasks, the users can read the explanation of each image to understand why it would be censored or not.

The language is willingly ironic and provocative, with a strong link to Instagram: the main elements of its identity are reworked to create a new language associated with the social media. In this way, the users feel part of the Instagram community and recognise the platform, despite the absurdity with which "Law of the Jungle" presents itself.

↑ At the end of the game, the users receive a profile based on their scores.

↑ The "policy" section contains all the questions at the base of the project to foster the users to further reflection. The gallery contains all the images that Instagram would censor.

↑ The "about" page explains the various step of the project and its meanings.
