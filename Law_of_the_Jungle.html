Law of the Jungle

→ visit the website

Instagram proposes itself as an open-minded platform, yet through the use of policies about sexual content, it denies its users' freedom of expression, reinforcing societal taboos. The ambiguous community guidelines often result in image removals or account blocks, leaving users unsure of what's acceptable.

“Law of the Jungle” is an experimental digital artifact aiming to clarify Instagram's community guidelines, spotlighting the aspects that could be unclear, ambiguous, or even discriminatory. The website is a game structured with four levels, each reflecting a section of Instagram's nudity policy. Through absurdity and irony, it highlights the policy's ambiguity. The project uses elements of Instagram identity, reworked to create a new yet familiar language.

“Law of the Jungle” is based on a strong provocation: What would happen if the Instagram nudity policy applied to the entire animal world? How would the platform handle the diversity of species, shapes, and behaviors? Users adapt to survive on Instagram, but how would the platform adapt to the vast variety of the natural world?

2020 — University project

Team: Antonella Autuori, Elena Catani, Mattia Cittadino, Anna Gazza, Barbara Vanoli

Faculty: Ángeles Briones, Gabriele Colombo, Michele Mauri, Simone Vantini, Salvatore Zingale

Skills: Visual Design, UX/UI Design, Coding, Game Design

My contribution: in this project, collaboration and comparison were integral. I contributed to various aspects including conceptualization, defining visual style, UX/UI design, storytelling, and resource research. Finally, I handled the design and programming of the “About” section.

"Law of the Jungle" consists of eight quizzes, two for each theme found in the guidelines, progressively unveiling Instagram's policy on sexual content. Players take on the role of content moderators, aiding Instagram in censoring inappropriate photos and identifying explicit images of humans and other animals. The ambiguity surrounding the multitude of images to censor prompts users to reflect on the topic.

↑ The tasks in each level are tailored to a specific section of the policy to guide users through them.

↑ At the end of each task, users can review explanations for each image to understand why it would be censored or not.

The language is deliberately ironic and provocative, closely tied to Instagram's branding: key elements of its identity are reimagined to establish a new visual language that's reminiscent of the platform. This approach allows users to feel connected to the Instagram community and identify with the platform, despite the overt absurdity of the presentation in “Law of the Jungle.”

↑ At the end of the game, users receive a profile based on their scores.

↑ The "Policy" section contains all the questions underlying the project to encourage users to engage in further reflection. The gallery showcases all the images that Instagram would censor.

↑ The "About" page explains the various steps of the project and its significance.
