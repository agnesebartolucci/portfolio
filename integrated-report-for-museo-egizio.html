Integrated report of Museo Egizio

The Museo Egizio in Turin, specializing in Egyptian archaeology and anthropology, commissioned Accurat to design its inaugural integrated report. The aim was to produce an innovative document highlighting the museum's value-generating activities. The report is divided into four sections, each characterized by a specific color of the brand identity. The colors are used again in the graphic elements within each one of them, to favor a strong characterization and visual clarity. Emphasis was on creating impactful, easily readable visualizations, often incorporating legends within the infographics and utilizing few, solid colors.

2021 — Professional project, done at Accurat

Team: Marco Bernardi, Davide Grimoldi, Alessandro Zotta

Client: Museo Egizio di Torino

Skills: Data Visualization Design, Information Design, Graphic Design, Editorial Design

My contribution: this report was my final project at Accurat, released months after my internship. I primarily contributed to the early conceptual and creative stages, aiding in collecting references, experimenting with styles, and outlining initial visual guidelines. My focus was on crafting timelines, tables, and initial visualization drafts, although some evolved in the final version.
