Hunty

Fear is an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm, and kids tend to embody them as monsters. However, kids do not fear what they know it does not exist, so they stop to be afraid of something when they understand it. Children have three main fears: darkness, loud noises, and nightmares. So, Hunty is an interactive, monster-shaped cape that helps children face their fears playfully by assisting them to understand their concerns through games and wearable sensors. The cap connects with an app that contains some stories about three silly monsters, each of them associated with one fear, that the parents could read to the children. The monsters would mess things up in every tale, but the situation gets solved by Hunty, the hero-monster with the features and the product name.

"Hunty" is a prototype; all the sensors are built with Arduino.

↑ The UI designed for the app.

↑ In the first three screens of out of the egg section of the app, the user can choose which story to read.

↑ In every tale, the monster is just afraid of something itself, causing the kid's fear, until Hunty, the hero, comes and resolves the situation.

↑ The eyes of the cape lit in the dark to fight the fear of the darkness.

When the kid is not wearing it, the cape goes inside the 3D printed egg to use it as a nightlight and speaker.

MY CONTRIBUTION
My role mainly involved the design of the cape and the choice of materials. In addition, I helped in UI / UX design and in the definition of visual identity guidelines.
