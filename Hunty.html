Hunty

Fear is an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm, and children often manifest their fears as monsters. However, children do not fear what they know does not exist, so their fear diminishes as they come to understand it. This IoT project aims to help children address three main fears—darkness, loud noises, and nightmares—that emerged through user research, in an innovative and interactive way.

"Hunty" is an interactive, monster-shaped cape designed to help children confront their fears playfully by assisting them in understanding their concerns through games and wearable sensors. The cape connects with an app that contains stories about three silly monsters, each associated with one fear, which parents can read to their children. In each tale, the monsters create mischief, but the situation is resolved by Hunty, the hero-monster, whose features inspire the product name.

2019 — University project

Team: Martina Galofaro, Eleonora Rossetti, Andrea Sabetta, Tommaso Tresso

Faculty: Federico Brugnoli, Franca Granzotto

Skills: UX/UI Design, Visual Design, User Research, Prototyping

My contribution: my main role involved designing the cape and selecting materials. Additionally, I contributed to UI/UX design and defining visual identity guidelines.

"Hunty" is a prototype, and all the sensors are built with Arduino.

↑ The user experience design we created for the app.

↑ Three possible user journeys modeled after the research we conducted, showing how and when different people could interact with Hunty.

↑ In the first three screens of the "Out of the Egg" section of the app, the user can choose which story to read.

↑ In every tale, the monster itself is afraid of something, which triggers the kid's fear, until Hunty, the hero, arrives and resolves the situation.

↑ The cape's eyes light up in the dark to combat the fear of darkness.

When the child is not wearing it, the cape goes inside the 3D printed egg, functioning as a nightlight and speaker.
