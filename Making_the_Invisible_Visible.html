Making the Invisible Visible

→ website

Online censorship consists of controlling or removing specific contents, accessible to the public, made by governments or platforms that want to regulate them. The latter's policies are becoming more numerous and detailed as the production of visual content grows exponentially. The more the phenomenon involves users, the more it obtains relevance, becoming one of the most discussed topics: why are some contents censored? Who censors them? Which is the boundary between appropriate and inappropriate content? This phenomenon can act silently, leaving the users unconscious, or at other times it can be overly evident, pushing them to search for ways to respond to or bypass it. The research shows how censorship is applied to images differently based on the typology of the platform and the content.

"Making the Invisible Visible" is a collection of eight interactive visualizations on the topic of censorship. The research has been structured following three levels of depth. The first explores the types of censored content and the different visual languages used to censor them. The second analyzes the reactions that censorship produces, both in terms of anti-censorship movements and methods users use to avoid it. Finally, the third investigates the most invisible aspect of the phenomenon: who is working behind it?

↑ Research question 01 - Map of the most mentioned places around the world censored on Google Maps.

↑ Research question 03 - Representation of the most censored artists and artworks on social media.

↑ Research question 05 - Analysis of self-censorship techniques used by erotic magazines to deal with Instagram policy.

↑ Research question 07 - Visualization of a human moderator's standard profile through the worldwide job offers on LinkedIn.

MY CONTRIBUTION
Once we established together the areas of interest and the identity guidelines, I mainly dealt with research questions 03 (fine arts) and 05 (erotic magazines). I managed the entire process, starting from the idea and the data collection, and arriving to the design of the visualization and the identification of the main findings.
