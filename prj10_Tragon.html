Tragon

The Dragon is the short story by Ray Bradbury that has been trans-mediated to create this project, an illustrated Advent calendar for children. The main theme of the story is ambivalence: people can interpret the same in a radically different way in the Middle Ages and the post-Industrial Revolution. For this reason, the calendar tells in the first half the story of two knights and in the second half those of two railwaymen; thus, the kid has to turn the calendar upside down on December 12th. The surprise under the box is the story, revealed day by day: the users have to frame the illustration with his mobile phone, with an app for virtual reality, to read the parts of the story and watch the animations.

A book explains the initial narration analysis, the ideas brought to this particular trans-mediation, and the design choices. It is divided into two parts; the first one describes the research, and the second one illustrates the trans-mediation. The layout has been done in such a way to be turned upside down to read the second part.

MY CONTRIBUTION
I helped in the concept making and a little in the illustration. But mainly, I worked on the visual identity (especially on the logo) and created the layout for the research book.
