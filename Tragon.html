Tragon

"The Dragon" is a short story by Ray Bradbury that has been trans-mediated to create this project—an illustrated Advent calendar for children that utilizes virtual reality (VR) to retell the tale. The central theme of the tale is ambivalence, as people can interpret the same event radically differently in the Middle Ages compared to the post-Industrial Revolution. For this reason, the calendar presents the story of two knights in the first half and two railwaymen in the second half. As a result, the child must turn the calendar upside down on December 12th. The surprise under the box is the narrative, revealed day by day: users must frame the illustration with their mobile phone using a VR app to read the segments of the tale and watch the animations.

2019 — University project

Team: Leonardo Codamo, Martina Galofaro, Anna Gazza, Eleonora Rossetti, Tommaso Tresso, Barbara Vanoli

Faculty: Matteo Ciastellardi

Skills: Graphic Design, Editorial Design, Illustration

My contribution: I contributed to the conceptualization and provided some input for the illustrations. However, my main focus was on developing the visual identity, particularly the logo, and designing the layout for the research book.

↑ The sequential scan of all the calendar boxes unveils the complete story.

↑ On the back of the calendar, users can find instructions on how to use it.

A book details the initial narrative analysis, the ideas behind this particular trans-mediation, and the design choices. It is divided into two parts: the first describes the research, and the second illustrates the trans-mediation. The layout is designed to be flipped upside down to read the second part.
