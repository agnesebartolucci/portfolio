ESC

→ book of the project

An interactive narrative is a form of digital experience in which users influence a storyline through their actions. The project aimed to create that using the transmedial translation of the novel Petronius written by Marcel Schwob. Hence, Esc is a Location-Based Mobile Game (LBMG) made of physical and digital media aimed to encourage university students to explore the university area, experiencing it as a lively neighbourhood with places to discover. The users use an app and a daily journal to enter the narration, interacting with it through meaningful choices that will lead to different routes and endings. The discovery theme, central to the original work, is reinterpreted using the escape as a metaphor that leads the users to explore the surroundings, interacting with the physical environments. This theme is repurposed and underlined, becoming the whole anchor point, that guides users to discover and enhance some areas of the city with small hidden gems that are often ignored.

"Esc" was developed to maintain the original values, topic, and main characters of the story, and creating new ones, thus expanding the narrative universe. The game adapts the novel to an alternate reality and retains the main characters by modifying their names in Petronio and Sirio. The medium of the journal translates Petronius' passion for writing. The main topic in the story is the desire to escape to discover something new, meet new people, and make new adventures. It is repurposed and underlined, becoming the whole narrative's anchor point.

↑ The character's cards provide supplementary information and help the users to relate better with them.

The story is set in Italy in 2020 after that an educational reform came into effect in 2002. The university acquired freedom of self-regulation, creating a system in which discipline, rigor, and seriousness are the central values. It defines the entire students' educational path and educates them to standardise their minds. This environment separates itself from the suburbs, where self-expression and diversity are shared values.

↑ The map inside the journal serves to facilitate the user in the exploration of the places.

The set of the game prototype is in Bovisa (Milan). Still, "Esc" is thought to be adaptable to several university areas through a multimedia narration supported by physical and digital media that connect the story to the physical environment where the users play. The game calls the users that play as the protagonist to interact with physical places through actions and artefacts that allow the narrative flow to continue.

The project is made of a system of artefacts starting from the advertising campaign. Then there is the journal that users must use to write useful information, solve riddles and minigames, contains a map that helps users find places and the rules that show how to use the provided media. Finally, the visual novel in the shape of a mobile app uses a geolocation system to access the various scenes, each with photos, dialogues, and multiple choices that lead to different endings. There are also additional contents not essential for the narrative. They add information that allows users to dive more into the story, such as newspaper articles, character cards, or Instagram profiles.

↑ Nearly every location has a riddle; the user needs to resolve to find the next place, but it is not meaningful for the story. Sometimes, instead, there is a minigame in which resolution is essential for the advancement of the narration.

↑ The advertising posters are meant to be displayed in the set of the game: the university and the surroundings to explore.

MY CONTRIBUTION
It was a lot of fun to work on ESC because we settled it in our university neighbourhood. I helped in the concept, in the storytelling, I developed and wrote lines for one character, and I have created all the routes in the neighbourhood. As for the graphic design part, I contributed to the visual identity of the project. Then I worked on all the artefacts related to the journal and the riddles, like maps, articles, or postcards, but also on the on-site part of things: I designed every poster, flyer, or sticker the user can find in his way.
